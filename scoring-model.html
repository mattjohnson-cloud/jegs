Signal Architecture
How JEGS Data Flows Into Ascend
Two live signal feeds — GSC and GA4 — cover the full customer journey from organic search through purchase. eCommerce platform and PIM integrations are out of scope for Phase 1. This page is the delivery team reference for what is connected, what it produces, and what is not yet available.

What Each Signal Covers
Google Search Console — Pre-Click
Organic Impressions
How many times each product page appeared in Google search results. High impressions with low CTR = title/content gap.
Click-Through Rate (CTR)
% of impressions that resulted in a click. Benchmark vs. category average to identify underperformers.
Search Query Mapping
What queries trigger each product URL. Reveals terminology gaps between how buyers search and how JEGS describes products.
Average Position
Ranking position in Google results. Position 1–3 with low CTR signals a title or meta description problem, not a ranking problem.
Google Analytics 4 — Post-Click
Product Page Views + Engagement
Sessions, bounce rate, time on page per SKU. High bounce = buyer landed but couldn't evaluate the product. Missing specs, images, or fitment data.
Add-to-Cart Events (SKU-level)
eCommerce event: add_to_cart. Requires confirmation that GA4 eCommerce tracking is firing at SKU level before Week 2 is signed off.
Checkout + Cart Abandonment
eCommerce events: begin_checkout, purchase. High ATC + high abandonment = trust or fitment problem at checkout.
Fitment Conflict Exits
Sessions where buyer selected a vehicle and then exited. Automotive-specific signal. Requires fitment selector event instrumentation — confirm with JEGS tech team.
Scoring Model
How Search, Find, and Buy Scores Are Calculated
Each SKU receives three scores (0–100) derived from live signal data. Scores are equally weighted in the current model. Weights are a delivery team decision and can be adjusted as we learn from the JEGS data — document any changes here.
Composite Score Formula — Current Model
Composite = (Search × 0.33) + (Find × 0.33) + (Buy × 0.34)
All three dimensions equally weighted — illustrative model for JEGS beta. Weights should be reviewed after Month 2 data is available and confirmed with Data Mo before use in any client-facing KPI report. The 0.34 on Buy is rounding — effectively equal thirds.
🔍 Search Score — GSC
Search Visibility
Gap: high impressions, low CTR
Primary input
CTR vs. Category Benchmark
SKU CTR measured against the average CTR for its category. A carburetor page with 2.1% CTR vs. 4.7% category avg scores poorly here.
gsc: ctr / category_avg_ctr
Secondary input
Impression Volume
Raw impression count. High-impression SKUs with low CTR get higher urgency weight in the priority engine.
gsc: impressions
Supporting input
Average Position
Ranking position. Used to distinguish whether low CTR is a ranking problem (position 8+) or a content problem (position 1–3, low CTR).
gsc: position
Score interpretation: 75+ = performing at or above category benchmark. 50–74 = CTR gap exists, enrichment will help. <50 = significant content gap — title, meta description, or structured data likely missing.
🗂 Find Score — GA4
Findability / Evaluability
Gap: clicks through but bounces
Primary input
Bounce Rate (inverted)
High bounce = buyer couldn't evaluate the product and left. Score is inverted: low bounce → high Find score. JEGS Engine avg: 72% bounce = very low Find score.
ga4: bounce_rate (inverted)
Secondary input
Engagement Time per SKU
Average time buyers spend on the product page. Longer engagement correlates with richer content — specs, fitment tables, install notes present.
ga4: engagement_time_msec
Supporting input
Fitment Filter Usage
% of sessions where buyer interacts with year/make/model selector. Low filter usage on a fitment-dependent part signals the selector isn't present or not working.
ga4: custom_event: fitment_select
Score interpretation: 75+ = buyers are engaging and evaluating. 50–74 = moderate drop-off, attribute gaps likely. <50 = buyers can't evaluate the product — missing specs, descriptions, or fitment data.
🛒 Buy Score — GA4
Conversion Readiness
Gap: engaged but doesn't convert
Primary input
Add-to-Cart Rate
% of product page sessions that result in an add-to-cart event. JEGS Engine avg: 5% ATC vs. 17% for Exhaust Systems — the gap is where the score comes from.
ga4: add_to_cart / sessions
Secondary input
Cart-to-Checkout Rate
Of buyers who add to cart, how many initiate checkout? High ATC + low checkout signals a trust or specification problem discovered at cart review.
ga4: begin_checkout / add_to_cart
Supporting input
Fitment Conflict Exits
Sessions where the buyer selected a vehicle, viewed a product, and then exited without adding to cart. Strong signal of missing or inaccurate fitment data — automotive-specific.
ga4: fitment_conflict_exit (custom)
Score interpretation: 75+ = strong conversion signal — SKU ready to promote. 50–74 = conversion gap, likely fitment or spec completeness. <50 = catalog content actively costing revenue.

Priority Engine
How SKUs Are Ranked for the Enrichment Queue
The priority engine converts Search, Find, and Buy signal gaps into a single Priority Score (0–100) per SKU. Higher score = higher revenue impact from enrichment. The queue is ordered by Priority Score, not backlog order. This is the core differentiator.
Priority Score Calculation — 5 Steps
01
📥
Ingest Signals
Pull GSC + GA4 data for all JEGS product URLs. Build per-SKU signal profile via URL crosswalk.
02
📊
Calculate Gap
For each SKU: measure actual performance vs. category benchmark across Search, Find, Buy.
03
🔢
Score 0–100
Normalize each gap to a 0–100 score. 100 = largest gap (most urgent). 0 = no gap (no need to enrich).
04
⚖️
Weight & Combine
Apply equal weights (33/33/34) to produce Composite Priority Score. High commercial volume amplifies score.
05
📋
Rank Queue
Sort all JEGS SKUs by Priority Score descending. Top 500 enter the monthly batch. Queue resets each cycle.

Completeness Scoring
How Attribute Completeness Gets Scored
Completeness scoring measures how complete each enriched SKU is against the approved attribute standard. Unlike the signal scores (which come from GSC + GA4), completeness is measured directly against the catalog data. This section has open decisions that require JEGS input and delivery team sign-off before production begins.
🚩
This section contains the most open decisions in the scoring model. We are doing the normalization work, but JEGS owns the taxonomy and schema. We do not have direct PIM access. The completeness model cannot be finalized until (1) JEGS provides or approves the attribute standard, (2) we determine how to maintain a working copy of the JEGS catalog internally, and (3) QA acceptance thresholds are defined. None of these are resolved yet. The open questions below must be answered before Month 1 production begins.
Completeness Scoring — How It Works When Resolved
The Basic Calculation
Attribute Completeness %
Completeness = Populated Required Attrs ÷ Total Required Attrs × 100
Example: a carburetor SKU has 12 required attributes per the approved standard. After enrichment, 9 are populated. Completeness = 75%. The 3 missing attributes are flagged in the delivery report for JEGS review.
Required vs. Optional Attributes
Required: Attributes without which a buyer cannot evaluate or purchase the product. For automotive: vehicle fitment table, core dimensions, compatibility notes. Missing = completeness penalty.
Recommended: Attributes that improve conversion but aren't blocking. Long-form descriptions, install notes, secondary images. Not penalized in completeness score but tracked.
Optional: Nice-to-have. Videos, 3D assets, cross-references. Not scored.
⚠️ The required/recommended split must be defined by JEGS per category. We cannot set this unilaterally.

Open Decisions — Completeness Scoring
Open Question 01 — Catalog Storage
How do we maintain a working copy of the JEGS catalog internally?
We do not have direct PIM access. To score completeness before and after enrichment, we need a representation of the JEGS catalog in our environment. Options include: (a) JEGS exports a full catalog CSV at kickoff and sends updates monthly, (b) we derive a working catalog from the flat files we've already delivered back, (c) JEGS grants read-only PIM API access for reporting purposes only. Each option has different data freshness, operational overhead, and contractual implications. This needs to be a design decision made with JEGS and Data Mo before production begins.
Owner: Data Mo + JEGS Catalog Contact
Open Question 02 — Attribute Standard Ownership
Who defines the required attribute list per category, and how is it kept current?
JEGS provides the taxonomy and schema. We do the normalization. The required attribute list per category (what "complete" means) must be jointly agreed during the Week 5 Enrichment Standards sign-off. We need to know: how many categories, how many attributes per category, and who on the JEGS side has authority to approve the standard. Changes after approval require a Change Order.
Owner: Pivotree Delivery Lead + JEGS Catalog Contact
Open Question 03 — QA Acceptance Thresholds
What completeness % floor must an enriched SKU hit before it passes internal QA?
Data Mo and Chantal Schweizer are required to define: minimum completeness % for a SKU to pass QA (e.g., 85% of required attributes populated), maximum exception rate per batch (e.g., no more than 10% of SKUs returned for client review), and what happens to SKUs that fail QA — remediate before delivery or flag for JEGS input. This is listed as OUTSTANDING in the Delivery Playbook (Item #2). Must be resolved before Month 1 production.
Owner: Data Mo + Chantal Schweizer · Due: Before Month 1 production
Open Question 04 — Pre-Enrichment Baseline
How do we establish the completeness baseline for JEGS SKUs before any enrichment?
To demonstrate improvement, we need a before/after view of completeness per SKU. This requires scoring the existing catalog state at kickoff. That requires a catalog snapshot from JEGS (see Question 01). The baseline is also the foundation for the Quarterly KPI Review — without it, we can't show progress. Propose: JEGS provides a full catalog export at Week 1, we score completeness against the agreed attribute standard as our Month 0 baseline, and we track delta each month.
Owner: Data Mo · Needed by: Week 3

Decisions Required Before Production
These are the unresolved items that affect how scoring and prioritization work in practice. Each one has an owner and a blocking dependency identified. None of these should be answered by the delivery team unilaterally — they require input from Data Mo, JEGS, or both.
Open Question 01 — Critical · Blocks Month 1 Production
How do we maintain a working copy of the JEGS catalog to score completeness?
We don't have PIM access. To score attribute completeness before and after enrichment, we need an internal representation of the JEGS catalog. Three options need to be evaluated: (a) JEGS provides a full catalog export at kickoff + monthly delta files, (b) we build our working catalog from the flat files we deliver back to JEGS (backward-constructing completeness), or (c) JEGS grants read-only PIM API access for reporting only. Option (a) is simplest operationally but depends on JEGS willingness and export format. This decision shapes the completeness model, the KPI baseline process, and the quarterly reporting format.
Owner: Data Mo + JEGS Catalog Contact

The Enrichment Standards doc (Week 5 deliverable) defines what "complete" means for each category. JEGS must approve this before production begins. We need to know: who has authority to approve (catalog manager, eCommerce director, or both?), whether approval is category-by-category or all-at-once, and what the process is for changing the standard after approval — each change is effectively a Change Order that affects QA thresholds and prior batch scores. Get this in writing at the kickoff call.
Owner: Pivotree Program Lead + JEGS eCommerce Sponsor

Open Question 03 — Critical · Delivery Playbook Item #2
What are the QA acceptance thresholds? Completeness % floor, exception rate ceiling, description length minimum?
Data Mo and Chantal Schweizer must define: the minimum completeness % a SKU must achieve before it passes internal QA and ships to JEGS (e.g., 85% of required attributes populated), the maximum acceptable exception rate per batch (e.g., no more than 10% of SKUs returned for client review), and minimum description length by category. Without these thresholds, the delivery team cannot make consistent QA decisions and client expectations cannot be set. This is OUTSTANDING in the Delivery Playbook as Item #2.
Owner: Data Mo + Chantal Schweizer

Open Question 04 — Important · Scoring Model Accuracy
Does JEGS have a fitment_conflict_exit custom GA4 event instrumented?
Fitment conflict exits — sessions where a buyer selected a vehicle and then left without adding to cart — are a high-value Buy Score signal for automotive aftermarket. If JEGS has this event firing, it significantly improves the Buy Score accuracy for fitment-dependent categories (Engine, Suspension). If not, the Buy Score relies solely on ATC and checkout rates, which are less diagnostic. Confirm with the JEGS tech or analytics contact during Week 1. If the event isn't instrumented, we can recommend they add it — but we cannot assume it exists.
Owner: Pivotree Data Team + JEGS Analytics/Tech Contact
Blocks: Buy Score accuracy for Engine + Suspension categories
Open Question 05 — Important · Score Model Calibration
Should Buy be weighted higher than Search and Find for the JEGS catalog, given the high purchase-intent nature of aftermarket auto buyers?
The current 33/33/34 equal weighting is the starting model. Automotive aftermarket buyers searching for specific performance parts (LS7 cam, coilover kit for a specific truck) are typically further down the purchase funnel than general retail buyers — they've often already decided what they want and are confirming fitment before buying. This suggests Buy signal gaps may deserve higher weight. The counter-argument: if a SKU can't be found (low Search), it doesn't matter how good the Buy score is. Propose reviewing after Month 2 data is available, comparing enrichment outcomes across weight configurations. Document decision and rationale before applying any weight change.
Owner: Data Mo · Review: After Month 2 data
Affects: Priority Score rankings · Enrichment queue order
